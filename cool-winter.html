The simple rule: If it looks warm, earthy, or golden — skip it. If it looks very muted, dusty, or greyed out, it may be pulling too far into Summer. Your sweet spot is cool, clear, and balanced. Cool always. Clear in everything else.

You sit between True Summer and True Winter. Cool Winter is a flow season, firmly rooted in Winter's cool, clear quality, but with Summer's influence softening the edges just enough. Your colours don't need to be as vivid as True Winter, and they won't be as muted as True Summer. When in doubt, lean towards Winter — it's your home season. You can carry more clarity and depth than True Summer, but you don't need the stark contrast of True Winter. The sweet spot is that dance in between: not so vivid it feels harsh, and not so muted you disappear into the colour. That balance is exactly where your best colours live.

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere: clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is expansive. The Cool Winter palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches here are carefully chosen examples to help you understand the qualities to look for: cool, composed, and balanced in depth and intensity. When shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit Cool Winter?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point — always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Makeup & Hair
Your Cool Winter guide to beauty. Cool, composed, and clear — with more depth and definition than True Summer. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, peachy, golden, or brown-toned — put it back. Cool Winter makeup plays in the middle: clearer and more defined than True Summer, without the stark vivid intensity of True Winter. Think cool berries, medium pinks, and purples with real colour payoff but without harshness. This is a confident mid-range that leans cool in every direction.
Lips
Your most flattering lip colours are cool-based and clearly pigmented — cool rose, clear berry, and cool mauve all sit in your mid-range sweet spot. You have room to go deeper and more vivid than Cool Winter, and you can carry more defined colour without it looking harsh.

Your "nude" lip — cool, not beige
Traditional warm nudes wash you out. Your version of a natural lip is a sheer cool pink, soft rose, or cool mauve balm. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or sheer berry. Try: ILIA Tinted Lip Conditioner Tula, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and clear roses bring life to your face without fighting your natural colouring. Blush should look like a cool, healthy flush — not warmth. Avoid bronzer entirely; it introduces exactly the warmth your palette doesn't need.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool or neutral undertone only. Pink-based or neutral foundations work best. Yellow or golden-based foundations will fight your natural colouring.
What to look for
Always look for a cool undertone first — this applies across every skin tone and depth. If you can't find a perfect cool match, a neutral undertone is an acceptable second choice. Avoid anything described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey — these will fight your natural cool colouring regardless of your depth. Most major brands carry cool and neutral options across their full shade range, from light to deep. For tinted coverage, ILIA Skin Tint and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up offer cool and neutral options across a range of depths. Tip: swatch on your jawline in natural light — a match that blends seamlessly is right. One that pulls orange, red, or yellow is too warm.
Eyes
Your eyes have real room to play in the blues, purples, and cool greys. Think periwinkle lid washes, clear lavender, steel grey, and soft navy — these sit right in your mid-range and bring out the cool clarity in your colouring. You can go defined and expressive here without tipping into True Winter sharpness. Skip warm browns entirely.
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: soft white, pale lavender, silver grey, cool rose-beige. Depth: soft navy, dusty violet, cool charcoal, slate grey, cool plum. Accents: periwinkle, clear lavender, steel blue, soft silver, dusty mauve. Satin and soft metallic finishes suit your mid-range placement beautifully. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal or cool grey is your most versatile liner. Navy is beautiful for a soft smoky effect. If black feels too sharp, opt for a cool graphite or soft dark grey. Avoid brown liner. Tip: a smudged navy or grey liner with a clear berry lip is a quintessential Cool Winter look — cool and expressive without being stark.
Mascara
Black or dark navy mascara is your best choice. Brown mascara introduces warmth and dilutes your natural cool contrast. Try: ILIA Fullest Volumizing Mascara in black, NYX Worth the Hype in black, or Ere Perez Almond Oil Mascara in black.
Brows & Beard
Brow & beard guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade darker. Look for cool taupe, soft cool brown, or cool dark brown. Avoid anything warm, golden, or auburn-toned. Tip: ask for "cool" or "cool-toned" when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. Cool Winter naturally spans a wide range — from light cool blonde through to cool medium brown and even deeper cool browns. Whatever your natural depth, the quality is the same: cool and ashy rather than warm or golden.
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: cool blonde, cool beige blonde, cool medium brown, cool dark brown — the full range works as long as the tone stays cool and ashy. Lighter options: cool blonde or platinum, always cool, never golden. Avoid: golden highlights, copper, red tones, warm auburn, caramel, or honey.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on Cool Winter. If you are going silver naturally, consider leaning into it rather than covering it. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and luminous rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or show them your palette directly. Tip: when in doubt, go cooler rather than warmer and brighter.
